Computers Timeline

1822 - Difference Engine

Invented by Charles Babbage, the Difference Engine was designed to perform mathematical calculations automatically.

1837 - Analytical Engine

The Analytical Engine, also designed by Charles Babbage, is considered the first concept of a general-purpose computer.

1890 - Hollerith Tabulating Machine

Created by Herman Hollerith, this machine used punch cards to record and process data for the US Census.

1936 - Turing Machine

Alan Turing's theoretical machine laid the foundation for modern computing with its ability to simulate any computer algorithm.

1971 - Kenbak-1

The Kenbak-1 is considered one of the earliest personal computers, capable of simple programming and data storage.

1975 - Altair 8800

The Altair 8800, launched in 1975, is considered the first commercially successful personal computer. Designed by MITS and powered by the Intel 8080 microprocessor, it ignited the personal computing revolution and inspired countless enthusiasts.

1976 - Apple I

Designed by Steve Wozniak and sold by Apple, the Apple I was a single-board computer and the beginning of Apple’s success.

1977 - Tandy TRS-80

Launched in 1977, the Tandy TRS-80 was one of the first mass-produced personal computers. Known for its versatility and affordability, it became popular in homes and schools, playing a key role in popularizing computing.

1981 - IBM PC

The IBM PC revolutionized the personal computer market, becoming the foundation for many modern PCs.

1982 - Commodore 64

Released in 1982, the Commodore 64 became one of the best-selling personal computers of all time. Known for its rich graphics and sound capabilities, it was popular among gamers and home users alike.

1984 - Apple Macintosh

The Apple Macintosh introduced the graphical user interface to personal computing, making it accessible to more people.

1990 - NeXT Computer

Launched in 1990, the NeXT Computer was designed by Steve Jobs' company, NeXT. Known for its advanced hardware and software, it played a crucial role in developing the modern web and influenced future computing innovations.

1993 - IBM ThinkPad 701C

The 1993 IBM ThinkPad 701C featured a unique "butterfly" keyboard that expanded for comfortable typing. Its portability and performance made it a popular choice for business users.

2001 - Apple iMac G3

Launched in 2001, the Apple iMac G3 was known for its distinctive all-in-one design and vibrant colors. It helped redefine desktop computing with its user-friendly interface and powerful performance.

2007 - Asus Eee PC

Released in 2007, the Asus Eee PC popularized the netbook category with its lightweight design and affordable price, making portable computing accessible to many users.

2020 - Apple MacBook Air M1

The MacBook Air M1 uses Apple’s custom-designed silicon, delivering significant performance improvements over previous models.
